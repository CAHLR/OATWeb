Conducting Rapid Experimentation with an Open-source Adaptive Tutoring System

Relevance Content Format Target Audience Meet the Team

Relevance and Importance

Intelligent Tutoring Systems have been an area of particular relevance and importance to AIED. In this tutorial, we introduce a new tool to accelerate the speed at which the community can innovate and experiment in the general area of computer tutoring systems. We showcase the field’s first fully fledged and open-source adaptive tutoring system (OATutor) with a completely creative commons licensed problem library based on popular open-licensed algebra textbooks. We demonstrate, with hands-on tutorials, how the system can be deployed on github.io and used to help AIED researchers rapidly run A/B experiments, including how to add or modify content in the system, analyze its log data, and link OATutor content to learning management system with LTI. Our open-sourcing of three textbooks worth of questions and tutoring content in a structured data format (JSON) may also open up avenues for AIED researchers to apply new and existing educational data mining and NLP techniques.

Format

Introduction (45 Minutes)

  • Introductions, Motivations (10 minutes)
  • Overview of the system (25 minutes)
  • Demonstration of the system [solving a problem] (10 minutes)

Research Capabilities Tutorial (1 hour 45 Minutes)

  • Demonstrate how to conduct A/B testing and analyze experiment data (40m)
  • Re-tagging content with different skills using ML (20 minutes)
  • Giving a personalized hint using a backend server (45 minutes)

Content Creation Tutorial (1 hour 40 Minutes)

  • Introduce content tools with short demonstration (35m)
  • Participants will create a problem of their own and view it in the tutor (1 hour)
  • Survey (5m)

LMS Integration Tutorial (45 Minutes)

  • Introduce LTI tool and demonstrate integration with Canvas (10m)
  • Teacher dashboard integration showcase (3 minutes)
  • Participants login as a teacher on Canvas and assign an OATutor lesson (30m)

Target Audience

Researchers who wish to conduct A/B testing with a tutor, educators who wish to create content for their classroom, HCI researchers, machine learning researchers, and adaptive technology researchers who wish to build upon the open-source tutor. Maximum participant count: 30.

Meet The Team

Zachary A. Pardos

Zachary A. Pardos (pardos@berkeley.edu) is an Associate Professor of Education at the University of California, Berkeley and a faculty affiliate in Cognitive Science. He holds a PhD in Computer Science from Worcester Polytechnic Institute. Prior to his faculty appointment, he conducted research in the ASSISTments lab and at edX as a postdoctoral scholar at MIT. He has 15 years of tutoring systems research experience and is widely published in the areas of knowledge tracing and recommender systems in higher education. His lab researches and develops high quality educational software projects, including AskOski, a course recommender system used by over 10,000 undergraduates per year, and an open-source python library for cognitive mastery estimation called pyBKT.
